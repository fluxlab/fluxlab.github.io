Flux

a feminist hackerspace in Portland, OR, USA

Welcome to Flux. We are working to create a space for a new culture of makers/breakers/fixers and benders in Portland, Oregon. A space filled with people and tools that can help you navigate both the technical and emotional hurdles of finishing your dream projects while also being friendly and non-threatening.

We recognize that the technology world is often a binder full of testosterone, and are working to make technology inclusive for people of all backgrounds and genders. We do our best to maintain an explicitly intersectional feminist space that welcomes members and guests of all genders, racial and cultural backgrounds, and levels of ability.

Technologies reflect the values of the culture that created them, we are focusing on creating a feminist maker culture in order to nurture new solutions that solve more problems than they create.?

We must make our space safe so we can be dangerous together!

Flux: is a hackerspace working to create a comfortable space for non-male people to make awesome stuff. All people can participate. In our hackerspace it is safe to be generous, vulnerable, and strong. We are building a source of strength to be ourselves in a community without armoring up against sexism and racism.

Guidelines for respectful interaction

  1. Be sober
  2. Be awake, go home to sleep, no one lives here.
  3. Clean up after your projects when you’re done.
  4. This is a space for making, not consuming.
  5. Treat other people as they would like to be treated. If you do not know how, ask them.
  6. Yes means yes, no means no, and maybe means no, silence is not consent. Accept “no”s without arguing, and say “yes” when you mean yes.
  7. Respect others’ personal space and possessions. Check in before you touch someone or their belongings. Don’t touch people’s hair, for example. We are not Npetting zoos.
  8. Respect chosen names/pronouns/genders, if you’re unsure ask.
  9. Act from an assumption of mutual respect and good will. Clarify misunderstandings, apologize for harm caused. Assume ignorance and not malice.
  10. Watch out for each other. Maintaining a respectful and safe community is everybody’s responsibility.
  11. If you’re not complying with these guidelines, you may be asked to leave by a Flux member.

Attend an open house every Tuesday. 7 o’clock.

Open houses are for you to see if you want to join. They’re open to everyone, including members, non-members and newbies at no cost. Bring a project you’re working on, an idea you want to ponder, or just come drink some tea and read something inspiring from our library.

I want to be a member!

Step 1: Visit an open house or schedule an appointment to meet us and gauge chemistry.

Step 2: Complete our Membership Application. Email it to INFO @ DA SPACE.

We do reference checks for our safety. Meanwhile, we recommend that you keep coming to open houses so we get to know each other for a couple weeks. In order for an application to be approved, there needs to be both a quorum of +1s and no more than one -1. That is, an applicant must both have the approval of enough of the membership and no more than one disapproval.

Membership Dues (plus $2 Paypal fee if paying online)

  • $50/month for small locker-BYOL
  • $160/month large locker and storage space

Membership Includes

  • 2 types of keys: keys to the door of the space and magnetic keys to the building. Everyone can get a key to the door of the space, but there are a limited number of keys to the building.
  • A key to the space and open access to the building front door 8-6pm and access when building key holders are present. You can always stay late, but the building doors shut at 6pm.
  • Free workshops when applicable
  • Invitation to share our discussion group and relevant docs
  • Permanent storage based on the size you pay for
  • Tne vote in proposals related to major space or rent changes
